Privacy Policy

SplitStupid is a small expense ledger for friend groups. This policy explains what data the web and iOS apps collect, how it is used, and how you can remove it.

Short version: SplitStupid stores the account and group data needed to sign you in, show your groups, split expenses, and share invite links. It does not sell personal data, serve ads, or use data for cross-app tracking.

Data We Collect

When you use SplitStupid, we may collect and store:

  • Account identity data from Sign in with Apple or GitHub, such as a verified email address, display name, provider account identifier, provider login name, and avatar URL.
  • App session data used to keep you signed in.
  • Group data, including group names, currency, owner, members, invite identifiers, created dates, and finalized status.
  • Expense ledger data, including payer, participants, amount, expense date, notes, edit records, void records, settlement checkpoints, and author.
  • Offline member names that a group owner adds for people who do not sign in.
  • Basic technical data that is normally sent with web requests, such as IP address, device/browser information, request time, and server logs.

How We Collect Data

You provide most ledger data directly when you create groups, join groups, add expenses, edit entries, settle, finalize, or share invite links. Identity data is provided by Apple or GitHub only after you choose to sign in. The app stores session data locally on your device so it can authenticate requests to the SplitStupid API.

How We Use Data

We use data to:

  • Create and maintain your SplitStupid account.
  • Authenticate you and protect private group ledgers.
  • Show group members, balances, expenses, receipts, postcards, and invite previews.
  • Merge Apple and GitHub sign-ins when the verified email address matches.
  • Operate, debug, secure, and improve the service.

Sharing and Third Parties

SplitStupid does not sell personal data and does not share data for advertising or tracking. Your group ledger is visible to members of that group and, where applicable, to service administrators who maintain the system.

SplitStupid relies on these third parties to provide the service:

  • Apple, for Sign in with Apple.
  • GitHub, for GitHub sign-in and GitHub profile data.
  • Cloudflare, for hosting the API and database.
  • GitHub Pages, for hosting the public web app and this privacy policy.

Retention

Account, identity, group, membership, and ledger records are kept while they are needed to operate SplitStupid. You can permanently delete your account in the app. Deleting your account removes your account and identities, groups you own, and ledger entries you created. References to you in entries created by other group members are replaced with an anonymous "Deleted account" participant so their remaining shared ledger can still be calculated. Some server or platform logs may be retained for a limited period for security and operations.

Your Choices

  • You can sign out at any time from the app.
  • You can delete groups that you own.
  • You can permanently delete your account and associated data from Settings in the iOS app.
  • You can manage Apple or GitHub account permissions from Apple ID settings or GitHub settings.

Children

SplitStupid is not intended for children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13.

Changes

If the app's privacy practices change, this page will be updated with a new "Last updated" date.

Contact

For privacy questions, open an issue at github.com/lfkdsk/SplitStupid/issues. Account deletion is available directly in Settings in the iOS app and does not require contacting support.
